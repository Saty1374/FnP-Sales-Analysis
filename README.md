## 🌸 FNP SALES ANALYSIS – EXCEL DASHBOARD & BUSINESS INSIGHTS

---

## 📌 PROJECT DESCRIPTION

This project analyzes sales data from Ferns N Petals (FNP) using **Microsoft Excel, Power Pivot, and DAX** to uncover business insights and trends.

An interactive dashboard has been created to track revenue, customer behavior, delivery performance, and product trends, helping in data-driven decision-making.

---

## 📂 DATASET OVERVIEW

- **Data Type:** Sales Transaction Data  
- **Domain:** E-commerce / Retail (FNP)  
- **Key Fields:**  
  - Order Date & Delivery Date  
  - Product Categories  
  - Revenue & Quantity  
  - Occasion-based Orders  
  - City-wise Orders  

---

## 📈 KEY KPIs

- **Total Revenue:** ₹35,20,984  
- **Average Delivery Time:** 5.53 days  
- **Average Customer Spend:** ₹3,520.98  
- **Quantity vs Time Correlation:** 0.003 (very weak relationship)  

---

## 🔍 KEY INSIGHTS

- 📅 **Seasonality Impact:**  
  Revenue peaks during **February and August**, indicating strong seasonal demand (likely Valentine’s & Raksha Bandhan).

- 🎉 **Occasion-Based Sales:**  
  Occasions like **Anniversary and Raksha Bandhan** generate higher revenue compared to others.

- 🛍️ **Top Products:**  
  Products like **Magnam Set and Quia Gift** are major revenue contributors.

- 🏙️ **City-Level Trends:**  
  Cities like **Imphal and Dhanbad** show higher order volumes, indicating strong regional demand.

- 📦 **Category Performance:**  
  **Colors category dominates revenue**, followed by Sweets and Soft Toys.

- ⏰ **Order Time Trends:**  
  Revenue fluctuates throughout the day, with noticeable peaks during specific hours (likely evening ordering behavior).

- 🚚 **Delivery Efficiency:**  
  Average delivery time is around **5.5 days**, which is acceptable but can be optimized.

- 🔗 **Weak Correlation:**  
  Quantity and delivery time show **almost no correlation**, meaning delivery delays are not dependent on order size.

---

## 🧮 KEY DAX MEASURES

- Total Revenue = SUM(Sales Amount)  
- Total Orders = COUNT(Order ID)  
- Average Order Value = Revenue / Orders  
- Average Delivery Time  
- Category-wise Revenue Contribution
